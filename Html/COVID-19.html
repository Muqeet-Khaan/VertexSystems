COVID-19

Overview

Introduction:

COVID-19 is an infectious disease caused by the SARS-CoV-2 virus. As of September 2025, test positivity rates are around 10.8%, with the XFG variant (Stratus) dominant in the US. Most infections are mild, but severe cases can occur in vulnerable groups.

Cause:

The disease is caused by SARS-CoV-2, a coronavirus first identified in Wuhan, China, in December 2019. Variants like Stratus (XFG) are now prevalent.

Types of SARS-CoV-2 Variants

SARS-CoV-2 evolves into variants of concern. As of August 2025, monitored sub-lineages include those under Omicron, with XFG (Stratus) gaining momentum.

  • First identified in the UK in late 2020.
  • Increased transmissibility by ~50%.
  • Associated with higher hospitalization rates.
  • Largely replaced by later variants.
  • Originated in India in 2021.
  • Highly transmissible and more severe.
  • Dominant globally in 2021-2022.
  • Reduced vaccine effectiveness against infection but still protective against severe disease.
  • Detected in South Africa in November 2021; Stratus in 2025.
  • Extremely high transmissibility but generally milder.
  • Many subvariants; Stratus spreading rapidly in US.
  • Current dominant as of September 2025.

Structure / Nature of SARS-CoV-2 Virus

SARS-CoV-2 is an enveloped, positive-sense single-stranded RNA virus in the Coronaviridae family. It uses spike proteins to bind ACE2 receptors.

  • RNA Genome: Single-stranded, positive-sense RNA (~30 kb).
  • Surface Proteins: Spike (S), Envelope (E), Membrane (M), Nucleocapsid (N).
  • Shape: Spherical, enveloped, 60–140 nm in diameter.

Transmission

Respiratory droplets and aerosols

Spread through coughing, sneezing, talking, or breathing near infected people.

Close contact

Direct contact with infected surfaces or people.

Fomites (surfaces)

Virus can survive on surfaces for hours to days.

Indoor crowded spaces

Poor ventilation increases aerosol transmission risk.

Asymptomatic and pre-symptomatic

Infected people without symptoms can spread the virus.

Common Symptoms

Fever

Cough

Loss of taste/smell

Fatigue

Shortness of breath

Headache

Sore throat

Body aches

Diseases / Complications

COVID-19 can cause mild illness but may lead to severe complications. Long COVID affects many, with persistent symptoms.

Mild / Moderate Complications
  • Pneumonia
  • Acute respiratory distress syndrome (ARDS)
  • Loss of taste and smell
Severe Complications
  • Multi-organ failure
  • Blood clots (thrombosis)
  • Long COVID (persistent symptoms)
  • Death
High-Risk Groups

Some people are more likely to develop serious complications:

  • Older adults (65+)
  • Young children (under 5)
  • Pregnant women
  • Immunocompromised individuals
  • Chronic conditions (obesity, diabetes, heart disease)

Prevention

1
Get vaccinated and boosted

Vaccines are the most effective way to prevent severe disease; updated for 2025 variants.

2
Practice good hygiene

Wash hands frequently and use sanitizer.

3
Wear masks in high-risk settings

Especially indoors or around vulnerable people.

4
Maintain physical distance

Stay at least 6 feet from others when possible.

5
Improve ventilation

Open windows or use air filters indoors.

6
Stay home when sick

Isolate if symptomatic or positive.

Treatment

General Care
  • Rest and monitor symptoms 🛌
  • Stay hydrated and eat nutritious food 💧
  • Over-the-counter meds for fever and pain 💊
Antiviral Medications

Antivirals like Paxlovid or Remdesivir for high-risk patients, started early. Updated for 2025 variants.

Severe Cases

Hospitalization for oxygen support, ventilators, or ECMO in critical cases.

  • Oxygen therapy
  • IV fluids and steroids
  • Close monitoring in ICU
Consult a doctor for personalized treatment; vaccines reduce need for severe care.
