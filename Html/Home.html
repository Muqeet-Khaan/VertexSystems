Introduction

Welcome to the Vertex System

Viruses are microscopic infectious agents that can only replicate inside the living cells of other organisms. They are made up of genetic material, either DNA or RNA, enclosed in a protein coat. Unlike bacteria, viruses cannot survive or reproduce on their own—they need to hijack a host’s cellular machinery to multiply. Viruses cause a wide range of diseases in humans, animals, and plants, from the common cold to more serious illnesses like influenza, HIV, and COVID-19. Understanding how viruses spread and affect the body is crucial for preventing infections and developing treatments. Common preventive measures include vaccination, practicing good hygiene, and avoiding close contact with infected individuals. Ongoing scientific research continues to improve our knowledge of viruses, leading to better antiviral drugs and vaccines to protect global health.

Types Of Viruses

A categorized guide to common human viruses.

Respiratory Viruses

These viruses primarily affect the respiratory system, spreading through droplets from coughing, sneezing, or talking.

Influenza.
(Flu):

Causes seasonal flu with symptoms like fever, cough, and muscle aches. Can lead to severe complications in vulnerable populations.

Learn More

COVID-19.
(SARS-CoV-2):

Responsible for the global pandemic, causing symptoms from mild respiratory issues to severe pneumonia.

Learn More

Common Cold.
(Rhinoviruses, Coronaviruses):

Leads to mild symptoms like runny nose, sore throat, and cough; highly contagious but usually self-limiting.

Learn More

Bloodborne Viruses

These viruses are transmitted through blood or bodily fluids, often causing chronic or severe systemic infections.

HIV.
(Human Immunodeficiency Virus):

Attacks the immune system, leading to AIDS if untreated. Spread through blood, sexual contact, or from mother to child.

Learn More

Hepatitis.
(Hepatitis B):

Affects the liver, causing acute or chronic inflammation. Transmitted via blood, sexual contact, or contaminated needles.

Learn More

Hepatitis.
(Hepatitis C):

Liver-targeting virus, often chronic, spread primarily through blood exposure, such as sharing needles.

Learn More

Skin Viruses

These viruses primarily affect the skin or mucous membranes, causing visible lesions or warts.

Herpes.
(Herpes Simplex Virus):

Causes oral or genital herpes, with recurring sores. Includes HSV-1 (cold sores) and HSV-2 (genital herpes).

Learn More

HPV.
(Human Papillomavirus):

Leads to warts or, in some strains, cancers like cervical cancer. Preventable through vaccination.

Learn More

VZV.
(Varicella-Zoster Virus):

VZV causes initial chickenpox (varicella) as a widespread rash in childhood, then lies dormant in nerves before reactivating as shingles (herpes zoster) later in life.

Learn More
