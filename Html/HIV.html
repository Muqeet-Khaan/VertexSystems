HIV (Human Immunodeficiency Virus)

Overview

Introduction:

HIV attacks the immune system, targeting CD4 cells, leading to AIDS if untreated. As of 2025, 39 million people live with HIV globally, with 77% on treatment; new long-acting drugs like lenacapavir offer hope.

Cause:

Caused by HIV-1 (global) and HIV-2 (less transmissible, West Africa); retroviruses that integrate into host DNA.

Types of HIV

Two main types; HIV-1 dominant worldwide. Groups and subtypes vary by region.

  • Causes most infections globally.
  • Groups: M (pandemic, 90%), O, N, P.
  • Subtypes: B (US/Europe), C (Africa/Asia).
  • High transmissibility; progresses to AIDS without treatment.
  • Less common, mainly West Africa.
  • Slower progression to AIDS.
  • Lower transmissibility.
  • ART less effective in some cases.

Structure / Nature of HIV Virus

HIV is an enveloped RNA retrovirus in Retroviridae family; uses reverse transcriptase to integrate into host genome.

  • RNA Genome: Two single-stranded positive RNA strands.
  • Surface Proteins: gp120, gp41 for entry.
  • Shape: Spherical, enveloped, ~100 nm.

Transmission

Bodily fluids

Blood, semen, vaginal fluids, breast milk.

Unprotected sex

Vaginal, anal, oral (lower risk).

Shared needles

Injection drug use highest risk.

Mother-to-child

During pregnancy, birth, breastfeeding.

Not casual contact

No spread by hugging, shaking hands, or air.

Common Symptoms

Fever

Rash

Sore throat

Fatigue

Swollen lymph nodes

Muscle aches

Headache

Weight loss (advanced)

Diseases / Complications

Untreated HIV leads to AIDS; with ART, life expectancy near normal. Opportunistic infections common in advanced stages.

Early / Moderate Complications
  • Flu-like acute syndrome
  • Oral thrush
  • Recurrent infections
Severe Complications (AIDS)
  • Pneumocystis pneumonia
  • Kaposi's sarcoma
  • Tuberculosis
  • Dementia
High-Risk Groups

More likely to acquire or progress:

  • Men who have sex with men
  • Injection drug users
  • Sex workers
  • Infants of HIV+ mothers
  • Sub-Saharan Africa residents

Prevention

1
Use condoms

Consistent use reduces risk by 80-90%.

2
PrEP (pre-exposure prophylaxis)

Daily pill for high-risk individuals.

3
Clean needles

Exchange programs reduce sharing.

4
Prevent mother-to-child

ART during pregnancy reduces risk to <1%.

5
Regular testing

Know status; treat early.

6
U=U (Undetectable = Untransmittable)

ART suppresses virus, prevents transmission.

Treatment

General Care
  • Regular monitoring of CD4 and viral load 🩺
  • Healthy lifestyle, vaccinations 💊
  • Prevent opportunistic infections
Antiretroviral Therapy (ART)

Lifelong daily pills or long-acting injectables (e.g., lenacapavir 2025). Suppresses virus to undetectable levels.

Severe Cases

Treat AIDS complications with antibiotics, antifungals; ART restores immune function.

  • Combination therapy (3+ drugs)
  • Adherence key to success
  • 95-95-95 goals by 2025
No cure, but treatment allows normal life; start early.
