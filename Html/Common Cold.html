Common Cold

Overview

Introduction:

The common cold is a viral infection of the upper respiratory tract. As of 2025, respiratory illness levels are very low, but colds affect adults 2-3 times per year and children up to 8 times. Over 200 viruses cause it, lasting less than a week.

Cause:

Caused by over 200 viruses, primarily rhinoviruses (30-80%), coronaviruses, adenoviruses, RSV, parainfluenza, and enteroviruses.

Types of Common Cold Viruses

Multiple viruses cause colds; rhinoviruses are most common. No major variants, but seasonal circulation.

  • Most common cause (30-80% of colds).
  • Over 100 serotypes; high mutation rate.
  • Peaks in early fall and spring.
  • Mild symptoms, self-limiting.
  • 10-15% of colds; 4 common types (229E, NL63, OC43, HKU1).
  • Seasonal, winter peak.
  • Milder than SARS-CoV-2.
  • Can cause lower respiratory issues in children.
  • Common in children; adults get milder colds.
  • Winter season; bronchiolitis in infants.
  • Vaccine available for high-risk 2025.
  • Global surveillance ongoing.

Structure / Nature of Common Cold Viruses

Varied structures; rhinovirus is non-enveloped RNA picornavirus. Small size allows easy transmission.

  • Genome: Single-stranded RNA (most).
  • Surface: Non-enveloped (rhinovirus) or enveloped (coronaviruses).
  • Shape: Icosahedral, 20–30 nm for rhinovirus.

Transmission

Respiratory droplets

From coughing, sneezing, or talking.

Direct contact

Handshakes or touching contaminated objects.

Self-inoculation

Touching nose/eyes after surface contact.

Crowded indoor spaces

Schools, offices increase spread.

Asymptomatic spread

Possible in early infection stages.

Common Symptoms

Runny nose

Sneezing

Sore throat

Cough

Mild fever

Headache

Fatigue

Watery eyes

Diseases / Complications

Colds are usually mild but can lead to secondary infections. Rarely severe in healthy adults.

Mild / Moderate Complications
  • Sinusitis
  • Ear infections (otitis media)
  • Bronchitis
Severe Complications (Rare)
  • Pneumonia in vulnerable
  • Asthma exacerbation
  • Dehydration in children
High-Risk Groups

More likely to develop complications:

  • Young children (<5 years)
  • Older adults (65+)
  • People with asthma/COPD
  • Immunocompromised
  • Chronic heart/lung disease

Prevention

1
Frequent handwashing

Wash with soap for 20 seconds or use sanitizer.

2
Avoid touching face

Keep hands away from nose, eyes, mouth.

3
Cover coughs/sneezes

Use tissue or elbow; dispose properly.

4
Avoid close contact

Stay away from sick people.

5
Disinfect surfaces

Clean high-touch areas regularly.

6
Stay home when sick

Rest to prevent spreading.

Treatment

General Care
  • Rest and fluids 🛌💧
  • Humidifier for congestion
  • OTC decongestants, pain relievers 💊
Antiviral Medications

No specific antivirals; supportive care only. Antibiotics ineffective against viruses.

Severe Cases

Rare; treat secondary bacterial infections if needed.

  • Monitor for worsening
  • Seek medical help if high fever or breathing issues
Colds resolve in 7-10 days; see doctor if symptoms persist.

Quick Facts

Discovered:
1950s (rhinovirus)

Family:
Picornaviridae (rhino)

Genome:
RNA Virus

Vaccine:
None available

History of Common Cold

Ancient Times

Described by Hippocrates as "catarrh"; known globally.

1956

Rhinovirus isolated, major cause identified.

1960s-2025

Vaccine trials failed due to diversity; focus on prevention.

2025

CDC monitors respiratory viruses; low activity in September.

Ongoing

Research into broad-spectrum antivirals continues.
