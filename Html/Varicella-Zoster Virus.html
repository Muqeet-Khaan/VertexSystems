VZV (Varicella-Zoster Virus)

Overview

Introduction:

VZV causes chickenpox (primary) and shingles (reactivation); highly contagious. 2025: two-dose vaccine recommended; new UK schedule from Sep 2025. Increase in adult CNS infections since 2019.

Cause:

Single virus; DNA herpesvirus causing two diseases. Latent in dorsal root ganglia.

Types of VZV

One type with strains; clade differences in severity. Vaccine strains attenuated.

  • Primary infection; highly contagious rash.
  • Childhood disease; 90% lifetime risk pre-vaccine.
  • Vaccine 1995; two doses 97% effective.
  • Complications in adults/immunocompromised.
  • Reactivation in 1/3 over 50.
  • Painful unilateral rash.
  • Shingrix vaccine 90% effective; two doses.
  • Postherpetic neuralgia in 10-18%.

Structure / Nature of VZV Virus

Enveloped DNA herpesvirus; similar to HSV. Latent after primary infection.

  • DNA Genome: Linear double-stranded DNA (~125 kb).
  • Surface Proteins: Glycoproteins gB, gH for entry.
  • Shape: Enveloped icosahedral, 180-200 nm.

Transmission

Airborne droplets

Chickenpox: respiratory route.

Direct contact with rash

Lesion fluid for both diseases.

Congenital (rare)

Transplacental for chickenpox.

Shingles not contagious

Only causes chickenpox in susceptible.

Highly contagious

1-2 days before rash to crusting.

Common Symptoms

Itchy rash

Fever

Fatigue

Headache

Loss of appetite

Nerve pain (shingles)

Skin sensitivity

Blisters

Diseases / Complications

Chickenpox mild in kids; shingles painful. Vaccine reduces risk 90%. 2025: rise in adult CNS cases.

Mild / Common
  • Bacterial skin infection
  • Scarring
  • Postherpetic itch
Severe Complications
  • Pneumonia
  • Encephalitis
  • Postherpetic neuralgia
  • Neonatal varicella
High-Risk Groups

More likely to have severe disease:

  • Adults (vs children)
  • Newborns/pregnant
  • Immunocompromised
  • Smokers (shingles)
  • Over 50 (shingles risk)

Prevention

1
Childhood vaccine

Two doses Varivax for chickenpox.

2
Shingles vaccine

Shingrix two doses for 50+.

3
Isolate contagious

Until lesions crust over.

4
Hand hygiene

Avoid lesion contact.

5
Avoid unvaccinated

Protect vulnerable.

6
Post-exposure prophylaxis

Vaccine within 3-5 days for susceptible.

Treatment

General Care
  • Calamine lotion, oatmeal baths for itch 🛁
  • Acetaminophen for fever/pain 💊
  • Keep nails short to prevent scratching
Antiviral Medications

Acyclovir, valacyclovir for severe chickenpox/shingles; start within 72h. Reduces duration/pain.

Severe Cases

Hospital IV antivirals, antibiotics for secondary; pain management for PHN.

  • Supportive for mild
  • Vaccine prevents most
See doctor if high-risk or complications.

History of VZV

1888

Chickenpox described as distinct from smallpox.

1958

VZV isolated; link to shingles.

1970s

Vaccine developed; US approval 1995.

2017

Shingrix approved; 90% effective.

2025

Rise in adult CNS infections; new schedules.
